Glossary

Definitions of key terms used in Fateh ERP and Saudi VAT compliance.

TermDefinition
ClearanceZATCA's real-time approval process for B2B invoices. An invoice must be cleared before it is legally valid.
Credit NoteA document that reduces the amount owed on a previous invoice. Used for refunds or corrections.
Debit NoteA document that increases the amount owed on a previous invoice.
DoctypeA type of document in Fateh ERP (e.g., Customer, Sales Invoice, Item). Each doctype has its own form and list view.
GCCGulf Cooperation Council — Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, Qatar, Bahrain, Oman.
Grand TotalThe total amount of an invoice including VAT.
Input VATVAT you paid on purchases. This can be deducted from your output VAT when filing your VAT return.
Net TotalThe total amount before VAT is added.
Output VATVAT you collected on sales. This is what you owe to ZATCA.
Phase 2The Integration Phase of ZATCA's e-invoicing mandate. Requires real-time clearance and reporting via API.
QR CodeA scannable barcode on ZATCA invoices containing invoice details and seller VAT number.
SARSaudi Riyal — the currency for all VAT reporting in Saudi Arabia.
Simplified InvoiceA B2C invoice (to end consumers) that requires reporting but not clearance.
Standard InvoiceA B2B invoice (between VAT-registered entities) that requires real-time ZATCA clearance.
Tax ID / VAT No.The 15-digit VAT registration number issued by ZATCA. Required on all B2B invoices.
UBL 2.1Universal Business Language v2.1 — the XML standard ZATCA requires for e-invoices.
UOMUnit of Measure (e.g., Nos, Kg, Box, Litre).
VATValue Added Tax — currently 15% in Saudi Arabia.
ZATCAZakat, Tax and Customs Authority — the Saudi tax authority responsible for VAT and e-invoicing.
