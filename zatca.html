ZATCA e-Invoicing

Saudi Arabia requires all VAT-registered businesses to submit invoices electronically to ZATCA. Fateh ERP does this automatically when you submit an invoice.

You don't need to do anything special. Just create and submit your invoices normally in Fateh ERP. The ZATCA submission, XML generation, digital signature, and QR code are all handled behind the scenes.

What happens when you submit an invoice

1

You click Submit

Fateh locks the invoice and starts the ZATCA process automatically.

2

Invoice is signed and sent

Fateh generates the required XML format, signs it with your ZATCA certificate, and sends it to ZATCA's servers.

3

ZATCA clears or reports it

For B2B invoices (business customers), ZATCA responds within seconds with a Cleared status. For B2C invoices (individual customers), it's Reported.

4

QR code is added to the invoice

The printed invoice PDF includes a ZATCA QR code that customers and auditors can scan to verify the invoice.

B2B vs B2C — what's the difference?

B2B (business customer)B2C (individual customer)
VAT number required?✅ Yes — customer's VAT no.❌ Not required
ZATCA processClearance (real-time)Reporting (within 24h)
Invoice type in FatehCredit InvoiceCash Invoice or Credit Invoice

What if my invoice fails ZATCA?

If there's a problem, Fateh will show an error message on the invoice. The most common reasons are:

  • Missing VAT number — B2B invoices must have the customer's 15-digit VAT number. Add it to the customer record and try again.
  • Wrong VAT number format — must be exactly 15 digits.
  • Certificate issue — contact Fateh support at support@fateherp.com.

To fix: cancel the failed invoice, correct the customer details, and create a new invoice.
