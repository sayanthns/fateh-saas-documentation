VAT

Fateh ERP handles Saudi Arabia's 15% VAT automatically. Here's what you need to know.

How VAT works in Fateh ERP

When you create a sales invoice and tick Apply VAT (15%):

  • Fateh automatically adds 15% VAT on top of the net amount
  • The invoice shows the subtotal, VAT amount, and grand total separately
  • The VAT is recorded in your accounts and included in the ZATCA submission

You don't need to calculate anything — just tick the box and Fateh does the rest.

When to apply VAT

SituationVAT rate
Most goods and services sold inside Saudi Arabia15%
Exports outside Saudi Arabia0% (Zero-rated)
Certain services (e.g., some financial, residential property)Exempt
If you're not sure whether your service is standard, zero-rated, or exempt, consult your accountant or ZATCA's official guidelines.

Input VAT (VAT you can claim back)

When you receive a purchase invoice from a supplier and record it in Fateh ERP with VAT, that VAT amount is recorded as Input VAT. This reduces the VAT you owe to ZATCA at the end of the quarter.

Example: You collected SAR 15,000 VAT from customers, and paid SAR 3,000 VAT to suppliers. You owe ZATCA SAR 12,000.
